Bio

My name is Peizhao Li, pronounced as 'Pay-Jow Lee' and written as '李沛钊' in Chinese. I am a fourth-year Ph.D. candidate at the Michtom School of Computer Science, Brandeis University, advised by Prof. Hongfu Liu. Prior to this, I completed my bachelor's degree at Beihang University. Throughout my Ph.D. studies, I had the opportunity to work as a research intern at Google Research, Adobe Research, Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories (MERL), NEC Laboratories America, and also as a research fellow at Harvard University. Starting in Fall 2023, I will be joining Amazon as an Applied Scientist Intern.

I research Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. I have worked on several topics such as Machine Learning Fairness, Multimodal Learning, Computer Vision, and Natural Language Processing. The title of my thesis proposal is 'Harmonizing Fairness with Utility in Data and Learning.' In my dissertation research, I develop methods from both computational and data-centric perspectives to make machine learning models fair and non-discriminatory, and simultaneously have no or minimal side effects on utility performance.
